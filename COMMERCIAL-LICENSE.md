## When a Commercial License Is Required

If your use is **commercial** — meaning it is primarily intended for or directed
toward commercial advantage or monetary compensation — you must obtain a
**separate written commercial license** from the copyright holder before
deploying, distributing, or offering the Software (or derivative works) in that
context.

Examples include, but are not limited to:

| Activity | Commercial license required |
|----------|----------------------------|
| SaaS or subscription product | Yes |
| Paid mobile or desktop app | Yes |
| White-label deployment for clients | Yes |
| Commercial hosting offered to paying users | Yes |
| Reselling or sublicensing HanQuran-based products | Yes |
| Embedding HanQuran in a paid product or service bundle | Yes |

Non-commercial self-hosting for a mosque, school, or community — without charging
users for access — generally does **not** require a commercial license, provided
use remains within HCCL.

When in doubt, contact the copyright holder before launching.

## How Commercial Licensing Works

1. **Inquiry** — Reach out via the official repository:
   [github.com/raqolbi/hanquran-app](https://github.com/raqolbi/hanquran-app)
   (GitHub Issues or repository contact channels).

2. **Scope** — Describe your intended use, deployment model, audience, and
   timeline.

3. **Agreement** — Commercial terms are defined in a **direct written agreement**
   with the copyright holder (Ramadian / HanQuran). Terms may cover scope of use,
   branding, support, and duration.

4. **Fees** — Pricing, royalties, and payment structure are **not fixed in this
   document**. They are determined case by case in the commercial agreement.
